What survived 45 hypotheses

Session filters, chart patterns, ETH lead-lag, funding dials, tie-rule tricks, stop-losses, hedges, timed exits, adaptive thresholds — all tested on 60 days (17,280 intervals), all dead. One effect survived hostile verification, twice, by independent re-implementations:
after an ISOLATED ≥12bps spikecalm hour before it (≤6 big moves)efficient last 30 min the next 5 minutes reverses ~55%
No train→test sign flip (the only overlay in the whole program without one), positive in all six 10-day folds, and it turned the one losing stretch positive. The cascade it excludes — fading moves inside a volatile run — is exactly break-even garbage. Alongside it, the research confirmed the cost model: what contrarian entries really cost (~48.6c weighted), and that ~a third of signals must be skipped, not chased, when the book prices them above 53c.

Impulse v2 — the new flagship LIVE ON PAPER

trigger: prior 5-min move ≥12bpsonly if isolated (gate above)buy the reverse ≤53¢ enter in the first 45s onlyhold to resolution — no stopsquarter-Kelly sizinga skip is a decision
Sizing runs off a learned win-rate estimate that starts humble and must earn confidence; if the edge dies, stakes shrink toward zero automatically (guard tiers + a hard bench). Two zero-risk shadow books run beside it — the same strategy ungated, and at the old 55c cap — so at day 60 we can prove, to the penny, whether the gate and the cap earn their keep. Verdict criteria were written down before launch: ≥ +1c/share with a confidence interval above zero = scale; below −0.5c = kill. Ambiguous counts as kill.
