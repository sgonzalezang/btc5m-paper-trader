₿ BTC 5-Minute — Momentum into Close · dual paper trader

Polymarket crypto Up/Down 5-minute markets · two engines side by side — strict 10/10 vs loose N/10 · read-only, simulated fills only
What this is. A live signal engine running two paper traders at once on Polymarket's crypto Up/Down 5-minute markets (BTC, ETH, SOL, XRP, DOGE). Both watch the same momentum-into-close signal — enter in the last ~2 minutes when the price has already moved hard, the crowd agrees, and the price still leaves value — but they differ in how many of the 10 guards must be green to fire: Strict needs all 10 (the disciplined strategy, rarely trades); Loose needs only N/10 (default 6, tune it above) so you see plenty of action and can watch how a trigger-happy version's P&L compares. Everything is fetched read-only in your browser (Gamma + order book + a Coinbase→Binance→Kraken feed). Neither engine ever places a real order: fills are simulated with a slippage haircut (live fills are worse than the screen) and settled against Polymarket's own resolution. Watch the core math on each ledger: your entry price is your breakeven win rate — the loose engine will trade far more but usually at worse prices, which is exactly the lesson. Ledgers live in this browser only (plus Export). Not financial advice.
Auto paper-trade Watcher is off — nothing is being fetched.
Under the hood. Markets are discovered by their deterministic slug <asset>-updown-5m-<unix start> (intervals align to 300-second UTC boundaries), with a series-feed and public-search sweep as fallback. Executable quotes come from the public CLOB order book (clob.polymarket.com/book), falling back to Gamma's bestBid/bestAsk; the spot price uses 1-minute candles from Coinbase Exchange → Binance data mirror → Kraken. If a direct call is blocked by CORS, a public-proxy fallback chain kicks in automatically. Strategy guards (ported from the "5min-btc-polymarket" momentum skill): 60–150 s left, confirmed move ≥ 0.10%/0.07% of interval open (≈ $100/$70 on BTC — percentage so it transfers across assets), momentum-side mid ≥ 52¢, ask ≤ 70¢, spread ≤ 3¢, top-ask depth ≥ $30, quotes fresh (book ≤ 8 s, feed ≤ 15 s), quote stable across two consecutive ticks (≤ 15¢ jump — a lone wild print is dirty data, not a fill), per-day trade & loss caps; plus a 0.25%/0.30% spot-retrace stop-loss and a $1+ micro-hedge when late skew goes extreme (≥ 95¢/93¢). Paper fills pay a configurable slippage haircut (default +1¢) on the visible ask. One simple strategy deployed across five assets beats a complex one on a single asset — switch assets with the selector and compare ledgers. Real execution would need an authenticated CLOB client, order signing, latency work and jurisdiction checks — deliberately out of scope for this page.
